How to Move Emotional Discussions Through to Resolution: A Harvard Negotiation Project Framework

The Harvard Negotiation Project's framework helps navigate emotional discussions by separating people from problems, focusing on underlying interests rather than positions, generating creative options for mutual gain, and using objective criteria to reach fair resolutions. By managing emotions constructively and building agreement incrementally, even the most challenging conversations can move from conflict to productive resolution while preserving relationships.

The Importance of Putting Kids First in Co-Parenting

Successful co-parenting requires parents to prioritize their children's well-being above personal conflicts by maintaining consistent routines, communicating respectfully, and never speaking negatively about each other. By keeping children at the center of every decision, parents can provide the stability and security needed for their children to thrive despite family changes.
