You're not sure
what to do next.

Things are different now, but the conversations haven't changed. Every discussion feels like the last one. You're exhausted, overwhelmed, and wondering if there's a better way.

The same conversation, again.

You thought you'd agreed. But here you are, back at the beginning.

It's not that either of you wants to fight.

It's that nothing is settled — and every small decision becomes a negotiation.

This isn't a communication problem

The ways you used to talk — the shortcuts, the assumptions, the patterns that formed over years — those don't work anymore. The context has changed.

It's not that you're doing it wrong. It's that there's no shared framework for making decisions together now that the relationship is different.

There's a difference between talking and deciding.

A place to start

Before you can stop circling the same conversations, you need clarity on the basics: schedules, expenses, decisions. Not forever — just a starting point you can both reference.

Parenting Plan Builder

Work through the practical questions on your own — custody schedules, holidays, shared expenses — and create a structured summary. Then share it with the other parent, an attorney, or a mediator. No account needed.

What clarity looks like

When the basics are settled, conversations change. Here's what becomes possible.

Predictable schedules

Know who has the kids, when, and what happens on holidays — without weekly negotiations.

Clear expense splits

Documented costs with receipts and agreed percentages. No more ambiguity about who pays for what.

Decisions that stick

Topics with clear resolutions, not endless threads. Agree once, move on.
