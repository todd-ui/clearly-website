Why co-parenting apps don't work

Most co-parenting tools are just messaging apps with a shared calendar. They give you a place to talk—but they don't change how you talk. The same patterns that made communication hard? They follow you right into the app. One misread text, one defensive reply, and you're right back where you started.

How it works

Structure that actually changes things

Clearly isn't a messaging app. It's a resolution system—designed to keep conversations focused on decisions, not drama.

Topics, not threads

Every conversation has a clear subject—"Soccer registration" or "Spring break plans"—and moves toward a decision. No endless threads. No relitigating old issues. Topics resolve, then close.

Say what you mean, without the edge

Before you send, Clearly can suggest a calmer way to say the same thing. You stay in control—but you get a second opinion when emotions are running high.

Your rules, always visible

Custody schedule, expense splits, response expectations—you enter them once, and they're referenced everywhere. No more "I thought we agreed..." because it's all written down.

Yes, it handles the logistics too

Shared calendar with your custody schedule built in. Expense tracking that knows who owes what. A place for documents, contacts, and everything else you need to share.
